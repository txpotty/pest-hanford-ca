Recognizing the Need for Wildlife Control

Knowing when to seek professional wildlife control in Hanford, CA is crucial to preventing further damage and risks. Several signs indicate the presence of unwanted wildlife visitors on your property.

Strange Noises in Walls or Attics

Hearing scratching, scurrying, chirping, or thumping sounds coming from inside your walls, ceiling, or attic, especially at night, is a strong indicator of wildlife activity. Animals like squirrels, raccoons, and rodents often nest in these secluded areas, and their movements create these noises. Ignoring these sounds can lead to significant damage to your home's structure and electrical wiring, creating fire hazards. Our expert team can identify the source of these noises and safely remove the animal, ensuring your home is quiet and secure once more.

Visible Droppings or Urine Stains

Finding animal droppings or urine stains in your attic, crawl space, garage, or even living areas is a definitive sign of an infestation. The type of droppings can help identify the species of animal present. Wildlife waste can carry harmful bacteria and pathogens, posing serious health risks to you and your family. It can also create unpleasant odors that permeate your home. Professional cleanup and sanitization are essential to eliminate these contaminants and restore a healthy living environment. Our service includes thorough cleaning and disinfection after removal.

Damaged Insulation, Wiring, or Belongings

Wildlife, especially rodents and squirrels, have a strong urge to gnaw and can cause significant damage to insulation, electrical wiring, plumbing, and even stored belongings. Damaged insulation reduces energy efficiency, while damaged wiring presents a serious fire risk. Discovering chewed-up materials or nests made of your belongings is a clear sign of a wildlife issue. Addressing this damage promptly prevents further destruction and ensures the safety of your home. We can assess the damage and recommend necessary repairs during our service visits.

Witnessing Animals Entering or Exiting Your Property

Observing animals entering or exiting your home or business through gaps, holes, or chimneys is a direct confirmation of an infestation. Noticing animals on your roof, in your yard acting unusually bold, or around your foundation are also potential indicators. Prompt action is needed to seal these entry points and prevent more animals from gaining access. Our exclusion techniques focus on identifying and securing these vulnerable areas, providing a long-term solution to prevent future intrusions.

Unpleasant Odors

Persistent, foul odors that emanate from specific areas of your home, such as the attic or walls, can be caused by animal urine, feces, or even deceased animals. These odors are not only unpleasant but can also indicate a serious health hazard. Locating the source of the odor is crucial for effective removal and sanitization. Our experts can pinpoint the source of these smells and eliminate them during the cleanup process, restoring fresh air to your property.

Concerns About Disease Transmission

Wildlife can carry various diseases that can be transmitted to humans and pets through contact with droppings, urine, bites, or scratches. Diseases like rabies, leptospirosis, and hantavirus are serious concerns. If you suspect wildlife is on your property, it's important to minimize contact and seek professional help to mitigate these health risks. Our humane removal methods prioritize safety for both the animals and the occupants of the property. We also offer comprehensive sanitization to reduce the risk of disease transmission.

Understanding Common Wildlife in Hanford, CA

Hanford, California, and the surrounding Central Valley region are home to a variety of wildlife species that can sometimes venture onto residential and commercial properties. Having a general understanding of these common visitors can help you identify a potential problem and seek the appropriate assistance. Our team is experienced in dealing with a wide range of species found in our area.

Squirrels

Squirrels are a frequent sight in urban and suburban areas. While seemingly harmless, they can cause considerable damage when they nest in attics or walls. They are notorious for gnawing on electrical wires, which poses a serious fire hazard. They also damage insulation and can create noisy disturbances. Preventing squirrels from entering your property is crucial, and professional exclusion methods are often the most effective solution. Don't let cute squirrels become costly problems.

Raccoons

Raccoons are intelligent and adaptable creatures that are often drawn to human habitats due to easy access to food sources like garbage cans. They can cause significant damage to attics, chimneys, and eaves when seeking shelter. Raccoons are also vectors for diseases like rabies and roundworm, making their presence a health concern. Their strength and ability to open things make them challenging to deal with without professional assistance. Our raccoon removal services prioritize humane trapping and relocation, followed by effective exclusion.

Skunks

Skunks are primarily known for their potent defensive spray, which can create an incredibly offensive and long-lasting odor. They often burrow under decks, sheds, or foundations, causing structural damage. Skunks are also carriers of rabies. Dealing with skunks requires caution and expertise to avoid being sprayed and to ensure safe removal. Our technicians have the knowledge and equipment to handle skunk issues safely and discreetly. Avoid direct contact and let our professionals take care of it.

Birds

Certain bird species, like pigeons and starlings, can become pests when they nest in or on buildings. Their droppings are acidic and can damage structures and vehicles. Nests can clog gutters and create fire hazards. Some birds can also carry diseases or parasites. Effective bird control involves identifying the species, removing nests, and implementing deterrents or exclusion methods. We offer solutions to humanely discourage birds from nesting on your property.

Opossums

Opossums are nocturnal marsupials that are often seen scavenging for food. They can take up residence under porches, decks, or in garages. While generally not aggressive, they can carry diseases and parasites. Their presence can be unsettling, and their droppings can be a nuisance. Humane trapping and exclusion are effective methods for dealing with opossums. We can help you safely remove opossums and prevent their return.

Bats

While bats are important for insect control, having them roosting in your attic or walls can be problematic. Bat guano (droppings) can accumulate and pose health risks due to the potential presence of histoplasmosis. Bats are also protected in many areas, requiring careful and humane removal. Bat exclusion involves identifying entry points and installing one-way devices that allow bats to exit but not re-enter. This service requires specialized knowledge and adherence to regulations.

Coyotes

Coyotes are increasingly present in urban and suburban areas, posing a threat to pets and sometimes children. If you are experiencing coyote activity close to your home, it's important to take precautions and consider professional assistance for deterrence or management strategies. While we primarily focus on animals that enter structures, we can offer advice and potentially assist with deterring larger wildlife from your property perimeter.

Snakes

While most snakes in California are not venomous, encountering any snake can be alarming. If you have concerns about snakes on your property, especially venomous species, it's best to contact a professional for safe identification and removal. We can help identify snakes and provide safe removal services when necessary, ensuring your peace of mind.

Preventative Measures for Wildlife Control

Effective wildlife control in Hanford, CA doesn't just involve removing existing infestations; it also includes taking proactive steps to prevent animals from entering your property in the first place. By implementing preventative measures, you can significantly reduce the likelihood of future wildlife problems and protect your investment.

Secure Trash Cans

Unsecured garbage cans are a major attractant for many types of wildlife, including raccoons, opossums, and skunks. Animals are drawn to the scent of food waste. Investing in sturdy trash cans with tight-fitting lids, or securing lids with bungee cords, can make your trash inaccessible to hungry critters. Storing trash cans in a locked shed or garage until collection day is even more effective. Removing this easy food source is a simple yet crucial step in deterring wildlife.

Trim Trees and Shrubs

Overhanging tree branches can provide wildlife, especially squirrels and raccoons, with easy access to your roof and attic. Trimming branches back from your home's exterior can prevent animals from using them as bridges. Similarly, dense shrubs close to your foundation can offer hiding places and pathways for smaller animals or insects like ants or spiders. Maintaining well-trimmed landscaping reduces potential entry points and hiding spots.

Repair Damaged Screens and Vents

Damaged window and door screens, as well as broken or missing vent covers on attics and crawl spaces, are easy entry points for a variety of wildlife. Even small gaps can allow rodents, bats, or birds to gain access. Regularly inspect your screens and vents and repair or replace any that are damaged. Ensuring these openings are secure is a fundamental step in preventing wildlife intrusions.

Seal Cracks and Holes in the Foundation and Walls

Cracks in your home's foundation or walls, even small ones, can serve as entry points for rodents and other small animals. Sealing these openings with appropriate materials like caulk, expanding foam, or steel wool can create a barrier. Pay close attention to areas where pipes or wires enter the building. Thoroughly inspecting your foundation and exterior walls and addressing any vulnerabilities is essential for long-term prevention.

Install Chimney Caps

Uncapped chimneys are attractive nesting sites for birds, squirrels, and raccoons. These animals can become trapped in the chimney or gain access to your home through the fireplace. Installing a sturdy chimney cap prevents animals from entering while still allowing smoke to escape. This simple installation can prevent significant headaches and potential fire hazards.

Secure Pet Food

Leaving pet food outdoors, especially overnight, is another major attractant for wildlife. Store pet food indoors in airtight containers. If you feed pets outdoors, remove any uneaten food immediately after they finish eating. Bird feeders can also attract rodents and squirrels; consider placing them away from your home or using feeders designed to deter squirrels.

Clean Up Fallen Fruit and Berries

If you have fruit trees or berry bushes on your property, fallen fruit and berries can attract various animals, including rodents and raccoons. Regularly cleaning up fallen produce eliminates a readily available food source. Keeping your yard tidy reduces attractants for wildlife and helps in overall pest management, including mosquito control by eliminating standing water in fallen fruit.

Prevention is often easier and less costly than dealing with a full-blown infestation.
